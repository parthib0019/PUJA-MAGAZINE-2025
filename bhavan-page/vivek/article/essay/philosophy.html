A Philosophical Lens on Durga Puja

Sayantan Pahari,Chemistry (UG 1)

0:00 / 0:00
   

Durga Puja, a vibrant festival celebrating the goddess Durga, can be interpreted through the lens of Western philosophy, offering profound insights into human existence and societal values

From a Nietzschean perspective, the festival is a powerful affirmation of life and the human spirit. The celebration, with its ecstatic rituals, loud music, and joyous pandal hopping, embodies the Dionysian spirit—a triumph of passion, emotion, and creativity over a sterile, rational existence. Durga Puja embodies Nietzschean will-to-power and the Absurd's heroic defiance. The festival's fleeting beauty and joyful rebellion against meaninglessness affirm human existence, transforming temporary idols into a profound celebration of life.

René Descartes's philosophy, particularly his famous dictum "I think, therefore I am" (Cogito, ergo sum), can be seen in the individual's reflective experience within the collective celebration. While the festival is a mass event, each participant's engagement is a personal act of faith and cultural connection.

Albert Camus and the philosophy of Absurdism provide a different interpretation. Durga Puja's pandals and idols, though temporary, are a powerful act of defiance. Their fleeting beauty and eventual immersion mirror life's impermanence, yet the community's joyous celebration in the face of this transience is a heroic embrace of the absurd. This conscious choice to find meaning in a shared, momentary experience affirms the present and rebels against a meaningless cosmos.

Durga Puja transcends mere ritual, embodying a profound spiritual truth. It is a divine play where the mother reveals her power and beauty in a fleeting form, only to withdraw it, reminding us of the ultimate reality beyond name and shape. In this Rapturous and Revelatory Revelation, the heart finds its own meaning, not in logic or rebellion, but in the unconditional love and surrender to the divine. This celebration is a joyful affirmation of the soul's eternal dance with the infinite, finding truth not in defiance, but in devotion.
