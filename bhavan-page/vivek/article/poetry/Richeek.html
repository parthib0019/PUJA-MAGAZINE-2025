Neon Durgati

Richeek Chakrabarti, UG-1, Department of English

0:00 / 0:00
   

Skyscrapers pulse in electric red,
Hover-lions prowl where humans once tread.
Durga ascends on a laser-lit throne,
Her many arms flicker like drones.

Incense clouds mix with ozone and sparks,
Robotic drums shake the neon-dark.
Digital rivers flood the streets below,
Where holograms of devotees bow in a glow.

Artificial conscious priests chant in binary hymns,
Laser flowers fall as the city dims.
The old world bows, the new world stares,
As Goddess triumphs through synthetic prayers.

Her eyes, diodes of dawn; her voice, a synth-song,
Yet courage and devotion still pulse strong.
In circuits and steel, tradition survives,
Durga Puja—futuristic, yet alive.
